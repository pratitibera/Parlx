FAQs

Enamels are oil based when the medium (solvent) that carries the paint resins, pigments, and additives is oil, and water based when the medium is water. In addition, the resin utilised depends on the solvent.
Oil-based enamels produce layers that are firm, long-lasting, and glossy. Oil-based paints, on the other hand, take longer to dry. While water-based enamels dry faster, they provide a less glossy finish than oil-based enamels.

A primer, sometimes known as an undercoat, is a coating applied to objects prior to painting. Priming improves paint adhesion to the surface, extends the life of the paint, and provides additional protection for the object being painted. Walls can be made of wood or metal. Primer promotes paint adhesion to the surface and extends the life of the paint.

Matte is a term used to describe coatings that lack the reflective and lustrous qualities of gloss. There are a number of matte options available when it comes to selecting and finishing kitchen cabinets. Paint finishes, for example, can be matte, which implies they don't shine.
